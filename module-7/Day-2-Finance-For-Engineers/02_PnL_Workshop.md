# Workshop: P&L Analysis & ROI Calculation
**Time: 3.5 Hours**

## Part 1: P&L Analysis (75 mins)
**Exercise:** Analyze 2 simplified P&L statements.

The instructor provides P&Ls for two fictional companies:
*   **Company A:** A SaaS product with growing revenue but declining margins.
*   **Company B:** A consulting firm transitioning to a product model.

For each P&L, answer:
1.  Is this company healthy? Why or why not?
2.  Where is money being made? Where is it being lost?
3.  If you were the CTO, what technical investment would improve the P&L the most? Why?
4.  What KPIs would you track to monitor the health of this business?

## Part 2: ROI Calculation (60 mins)
**Scenario:** Your team proposes automating a manual process that currently takes 4 people × 20 hours/week each.

Calculate:
*   **Current annual cost:** of the manual process (assume a loaded engineering salary).
*   **Estimated build cost:** team size × weeks × loaded salary.
*   **Estimated ongoing cost:** hosting, maintenance, monitoring.
*   **Annual savings & payback period.**
*   **3-Year ROI.**

Write a 1-paragraph executive summary: *"We should invest $X because it will save $Y over Z years, with a payback period of W months."*

## Part 3: Metrics Framework for Your Capstone (60 mins)
**Exercise:** Define the business metrics for your capstone project.

Using your Lean Canvas from Day 1, define:
1.  **Primary Metric (the ONE number):** The single metric that proves your capstone works.
    *   Example: "Time to resolve incidents reduced by X%."
2.  **Leading Indicators (3–5):** Early signals that predict success.
    *   Example: user adoption rate, daily active usage, task completion rate.
3.  **Guardrail Metrics (1–2):** Things that should NOT get worse as you optimize the primary metric.
    *   Example: user satisfaction shouldn't drop as speed increases.
4.  **Business Impact Metric:** What is the dollar value of improvement?
    *   Example: hours saved × hourly cost = annual value.

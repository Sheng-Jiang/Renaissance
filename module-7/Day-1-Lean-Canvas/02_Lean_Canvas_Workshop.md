# Workshop: Lean Canvas & Value Proposition Design
**Time: 4 Hours**

## Part 1: Lean Canvas for Known Products (60 mins)
**Exercise:** Create Lean Canvases for 2 products you use daily.

*   Pick one **consumer** product (e.g., Spotify, Uber, Notion) and one **developer tool** (e.g., GitHub, Vercel, Datadog).
*   Fill every block. Struggling with a block *is* the learning — don't skip it.
*   For **Revenue Streams**: research how the product actually makes money.
*   For **Key Metrics**: what would the CEO of this company check every morning?
*   **Pair Review:** Exchange canvases with a partner. Challenge each block:
    *   "Is the problem really a top-3 pain?"
    *   "Is the customer segment specific enough?"
    *   "Is the UVP truly unique?"

## Part 2: Lean Canvas for Your Capstone (90 mins)
**Exercise:** Create a Lean Canvas for your capstone project.

*   Start with the **Problem** block: what are the top 3 pains your target user experiences?
*   **Customer Segments**: Not "developers" but "mid-level backend engineers at companies with 50–500 employees who are on-call at least once a month."
*   **Solution**: What is the absolute minimum you could build in Weeks 9–10?
*   **Key Metrics**: What would prove this works? (Not "users like it" but "users accomplish X in Y% less time.")
*   **Structured peer critique**: Present your canvas to a group of 3–4 (5 min present, 10 min critique).

## Part 3: Customer Discovery Interview Guide (45 mins)
**Exercise:** Prepare for Day 3's customer discovery interviews.

1.  Identify 5+ potential interviewees for your capstone problem domain.
2.  Write an interview guide using Jobs-to-be-Done framing:
    *   **Background:** "Tell me about your role and how [problem domain] fits into your daily work."
    *   **Current behavior:** "Walk me through the last time you dealt with [the problem]."
    *   **Pain points:** "What was the most frustrating part?"
    *   **Current solutions:** "What have you tried? What worked? What didn't?"
    *   **Value test:** "If a tool could [your UVP], how would that change your work?"
3.  Schedule at least 3 interviews for Day 3.

> **CRITICAL:** Do NOT describe your solution in the interview guide. You're testing the problem, not pitching a product.

# Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

Customers don't buy products. They **"hire"** products to do a **job** in their life.

**JTBD anatomy:**
> When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].

**Example:**
> *"When I get paged at 3 AM, I want to quickly find the relevant runbook, so I can resolve the incident without waking up the whole team."*

---

# The Four Forces of Progress

For a user to switch from their current solution to yours, 4 forces must align:

| Force | Direction | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Push** | Away from current | "The spreadsheet is too slow." |
| **Pull** | Toward new | "This tool looks much faster." |
| **Habit** | Staying with current | "But I know how the spreadsheet works." |
| **Anxiety** | Fear of new | "What if this tool loses my data?" |

**Push + Pull** must be *greater* than **Habit + Anxiety.**

# The Mom Test

*   **Don't tell** people about your idea and ask if they like it. (They'll lie to be nice.)
*   **Instead, ask about their life:**
    *   "How do you handle X today?"
    *   "What's the most frustrating part?"
    *   "What have you tried?"
*   Facts about their **current behavior** are worth more than opinions about your solution.

# Exercise: Customer Discovery Interview Guide
**Time: Full Day**

## Phase 1: Mock Interviews (45 mins)
Practice in pairs before going live.

*   **Person A** plays the interviewer (using their real interview guide).
*   **Person B** roleplays a user who experiences the problem.
*   10 min interview + 5 min feedback.
*   Switch roles and repeat.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
*   ❌ Leading: "Don't you think X would be better?" → ✅ "How do you handle X today?"
*   ❌ Pitching: "We're building a tool that..." → ✅ "Tell me about the last time you..."
*   ❌ Surface answers: "It's fine" → ✅ "Walk me through specifically what happened last Tuesday."
*   ❌ Confirmation bias: Only hearing what supports your hypothesis.

## Phase 2: Live Interviews (3 hours total)
Conduct **5+ interviews** across two blocks. For each, document:

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Profile** | Role, company size, relevant context (anonymized) |
| **Current Behavior** | How they handle the problem today |
| **Pain Intensity** | 1–10 scale (behavior matters more than the number) |
| **Key Quotes** | Exact words that reveal emotion or frustration |
| **JTBD** | What "job" is the user trying to accomplish? |
| **Willingness to Switch** | Would they try something new? What would it take? |
| **Surprise** | What did you learn that you didn't expect? |

## Phase 3: Synthesis Workshop (75 mins)
Structure your findings using the JTBD framework:

1.  **JTBD Map:** Top 3 jobs your interviewees are trying to accomplish.
2.  **Pain Ranking:** Rank pains by frequency × intensity.
3.  **Behavior Patterns:** What workarounds are people using? (Workarounds reveal real pain.)
4.  **Four Forces Analysis:** For each interviewee, map push, pull, habit, and anxiety.
5.  **Quotes Board:** The 5 most powerful quotes (these go into your business case).

Validate or invalidate your Lean Canvas assumptions:
*   🟢 **Green:** Validated by 3+ interviews.
*   🟡 **Yellow:** Partially validated, needs more data.
*   🔴 **Red:** Invalidated (a successful discovery, not a failure — pivot your capstone).

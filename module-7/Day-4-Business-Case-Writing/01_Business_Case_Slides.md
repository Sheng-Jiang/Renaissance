# What a Business Case IS

A structured argument for why a business should invest resources in a technical initiative.

**What it is NOT:** a technical spec, a feature list, or a sales pitch.

**The key insight:** Executives read differently than engineers.
*   Lead with the conclusion: *"We should invest $X because..."*
*   Use numbers, not adjectives: *"30% reduction"* not *"significant improvement."*
*   One page if possible. Two pages maximum.

---

# The Anatomy of a Compelling Business Case

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **1. Problem Statement** | The pain, backed by customer discovery evidence |
| **2. Proposed Solution** | What the user experiences (not architecture details) |
| **3. Market Context** | Landscape, competitors, gap (from research) |
| **4. Business Impact** | ROI, hours/costs saved, revenue potential |
| **5. Investment Required** | Team, timeline, tech costs, maintenance |
| **6. Risks & Mitigations** | What could go wrong and how you'll manage it |
| **7. Recommendation** | Clear ask with a timeline |

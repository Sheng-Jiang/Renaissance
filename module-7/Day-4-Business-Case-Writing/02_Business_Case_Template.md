# Exercise: Business Case Template
**Time: 3 Hours**

## The Business Case Document
Use the following template. Target: 1–2 pages. Concise, evidence-based, executive-appropriate.

---

### Section 1: Problem Statement
*Ground this in customer discovery quotes and behavioral data.*

> "[Target User] currently experiences [Pain], costing [Quantified Impact]. Our discovery interviews with [N] users confirmed that [Key Finding]."

### Section 2: Proposed Solution
*Describe what the user experiences, NOT what the code does.*

> "A [type of tool] that enables [user] to [key benefit], resulting in [measurable outcome]."

### Section 3: Market Context
*Use your NotebookLM Deep Research findings.*

*   Existing players: [Competitor A, B, C]
*   Market gap: [What nobody does well]
*   Trends supporting this: [AI adoption, regulatory changes, etc.]

### Section 4: Business Impact
*Specific numbers, even if estimated.*

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Current annual cost of problem | $[X] |
| Projected annual savings | $[Y] |
| Payback period | [W] months |
| 3-Year ROI | [Z]% |

### Section 5: Investment Required
*   Team: [N] engineers × [W] weeks
*   Build cost: $[X]
*   Ongoing annual cost: $[Y] (hosting, maintenance)

### Section 6: Risks & Mitigations
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Risk 1] | Medium | High | [Mitigation] |
| [Risk 2] | Low | Critical | [Mitigation] |
| [Risk 3] | High | Medium | [Mitigation] |

### Section 7: Recommendation
> "We recommend investing $[X] over [timeline] to build [solution], which will deliver $[Y] in annual value with a payback period of [W] months. The primary risk is [X], mitigated by [Y]."

---

## Peer Review Checklist
When reviewing a partner's business case, answer:
*   After reading this, would I fund this project? Why or why not?
*   What is the strongest argument? The weakest?
*   Is the ROI credible? Are the assumptions reasonable?
*   Is this written for an **executive** or for an **engineer**?
*   What question would a skeptical CFO ask that this doesn't answer?

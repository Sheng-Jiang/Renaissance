# Exercise: Problem Discovery & Synthesis
**Time: 2.5 Hours**

## Overview
Your team has been handed an ambiguous business challenge (e.g., "Customer support routing is slow and inaccurate"). Your goal is to transform this into a sharp, bounded problem statement and define the Minimum Viable Product (MVP).

## Phase 1: Problem Deconstruction (45 mins)
Do not write any code. Do not sketch UI. Answer these questions as a team:

1.  **Who is the specific user?** (Not "people". "Tier 1 Customer Support Agents doing shift work.")
2.  **What is the precise pain point?** (Not "it's slow". "They have to check 4 different systems to know who to route the ticket to.")
3.  **What is the boundary?** (What are you explicitly *not* solving this weekend?)

## Phase 2: The Problem Statement (30 mins)
Draft your final Problem Statement using this format:

> "[Target User] needs a way to [User's Goal] because currently [Precise Pain Point]. We will know this is solved when [Measurable Outcome]."

*Commit this statement to your team repository's `README.md`.*

## Phase 3: Silent Solution Design (45 mins)
We borrow from the Design Sprint methodology:

1.  **Silent Sketch (15m):** Every team member individually sketches out a solution/architecture on paper or a digital whiteboard. No talking allowed.
2.  **Present (15m):** Each member gets 3 minutes to present their sketch.
3.  **Merge & Decide (15m):** As a team, combine the best elements of the sketches into a single, unified MVP architecture. Decide what tools and frameworks you will use.

## Phase 4: Task Breakdown (30 mins)
Translate the MVP architecture into executable tasks.
Set up your GitHub Projects board and assign the tasks for tomorrow's hackathon kickoff.

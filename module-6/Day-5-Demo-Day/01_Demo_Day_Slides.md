# The Art of the Demo

A great hackathon demo does not just show features. It tells a story.

1.  **The Hook (1m):** Show the human pain. Why does this matter?
2.  **The Live Demo (3m):** Do not click through slides. Show the deployed, working code solving the pain.
3.  **The Pivot (2m):** Show your user testing results. "We thought they wanted X, but they needed Y, so we changed it."
4.  **The Architecture (2m):** "We built this in 48 hours using React, Python, ClawSwarm, and GitHub Actions."
5.  **The Future (2m):** If this were a real startup, what would we do next?

---

# How to Survive a Live Demo

*   **Pre-flight check:** Is the database seeded? Are the environment variables set? Is the WiFi stable?
*   **The Contingency Plan:** If the network dies, have a 2-minute video recording of the working prototype ready locally on your machine.
*   **Never apologize for what isn't built.** Focus entirely on what *is* built and working.

---

# The Judging Criteria

1.  **Problem Clarity:** Was the problem well-defined?
2.  **Solution Viability:** Does the prototype actually solve it?
3.  **Technical Execution:** Is the architecture solid? Is CI/CD running?
4.  **Presentation Quality:** Was the demo compelling?
5.  **AI Integration:** Were AI tools used effectively and verified?

---

# The Retrospective

After the awards are handed out, the hackathon isn't over.
We must reflect.

*   How did your team handle the pressure?
*   Who stepped up to lead? 
*   Did you cut scope early enough?
*   Did ClawSwarm make you faster, or was the overhead too high?

You started this program building alone. You are now building as a team.

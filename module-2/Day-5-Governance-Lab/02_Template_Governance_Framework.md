# Template: AI Governance Framework for Engineering Teams

**Instructions:** Create a comprehensive Markdown document in your portfolio repository summarizing your approach to AI-augmented development. Ground every claim using evidence you gathered in your AI Bug Log. 

Use the following 5 parts as your structure:

## Part 1: Principles (What We Believe)
Write 5–7 core principles for how your team approaches AI.
*Example: "AI output is a draft, never a final product."*
*Example: "Test coverage is our primary verification tool."*

## Part 2: Workflows (How We Work)
Define the lifecycle of a task from prompt $\rightarrow$ generation $\rightarrow$ verification $\rightarrow$ merge.
*   Explicitly include your AI Code Review Checklist as the standard.
*   Define when human-in-the-loop review is mandatory vs. when it can be skipped.

## Part 3: Tooling (What We Use)
Create a tool selection guide.
*   When should an engineer use Claude Code CLI vs. Kiro vs. Antigravity?
*   Specify what automated quality gates must exist in your CI/CD pipelines (e.g., complexity limits, security scanners).

## Part 4: Training (How We Onboard)
*   What must a new team member learn in their first week regarding AI tools?
*   How should the team use the shared Bug Log as a training resource?

## Part 5: Measurement (How We Know It's Working)
*   How will you track AI-introduced defects?
*   How do you measure whether AI tools are making the team faster AND more reliable?
*   What are the red flags that indicate AI is hurting velocity more than it's helping?

# What is AI Governance?

Governance is not about slowing engineers down. It's about establishing the rails that allow them to go incredibly fast *safely*.

1.  **Principles:** What we believe about AI code.
2.  **Workflows:** How we prompt, generate, verify, and merge.
3.  **Tooling:** Which tools we use, and our automated CI/CD quality gates.
4.  **Training:** How we onboard new engineers into this new paradigm.
5.  **Measurement:** How we know if the AI is actually helping or hurting.

---

# Principles Drive Behavior

A good governance framework is opinionated and based on evidence (like your AI Bug Log).

**Example Principles:**
*   "AI output is a draft, never a final product."
*   "If you can't verify it, the AI isn't allowed to write it."
*   "Automation is for consistency; humans are for judgment."

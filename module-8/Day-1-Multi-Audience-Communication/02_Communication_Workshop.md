# Workshop: Multi-Audience Communication
**Time: 4 Hours**

## Phase 1: Prepare Three Presentations (90 mins)
Choose a real technical decision from your past modules. Create three 3-minute presentations of the same decision:

### Version A — Engineering
- Include architecture diagrams and performance data
- Discuss trade-off analysis (why this option vs. alternatives)
- Use technical terminology freely
- Level of detail: enough for an engineer to evaluate and critique

### Version B — Product
- Focus on **user impact**: what does this enable for customers?
- Include timeline: how long does this take and what does it unblock?
- Discuss dependencies: what else changes as a result?
- Level of detail: enough for a PM to prioritize and plan

### Version C — Executive
- **Lead with the ROI**: "Investing $X saves/earns $Y"
- One business impact, one risk, one timeline
- No architecture diagrams. No acronyms without explanation.
- Level of detail: enough for a VP to approve or reject funding

## Phase 2: Multi-Audience Presentation Showcase (150 mins)
Each student delivers all three versions (3 min each, 9 min total).

**Panel Roleplay:** Three audience members each represent a persona:
- 🔧 **The Engineer:** "Did I get enough technical detail to evaluate this?"
- 📦 **The Product Manager:** "Do I understand the user impact and timeline?"
- 💰 **The CFO:** "Would I fund this? Is the ROI clear?"

## Phase 3: Reflection (45 mins)
Based on feedback:
1. Revise your weakest version.
2. Start a **Communication Toolkit** document: templates and tips for each audience type.
3. Practice the 30-second elevator pitch for each audience.
